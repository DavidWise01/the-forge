Thirty-two fixed beams. One white well.

This is a clean 1:1 simulation of the concept: sixteen became thirty-two. The emitters do not orbit. They are fixed to a doubled quadrant lattice and fire inward on every .01 phase tick.

The center is not a passive dot. It is a capacitance well: it accumulates incoming chromatic energy, saturates toward white, then vents controlled coherence through a visible pulse.

kernel: LOCK_32 → STEP(.01) → FIRE_CENTER → ACCUMULATE_17 → CONTAIN(98%) → RELEASE_EXCESS → REPORT

Geometry

Four quadrants. Eight emitters per quadrant. Each emitter has a fixed position, fixed color, and a phase offset. All vectors terminate at the same point: P17, the central white capacitance well.

Timing

Every animation tick advances the model by .01. The phase controls beam intensity, not position. This keeps the cavity physically readable: fixed hardware, variable field.

Containment

The well retains most incoming energy. Excess energy is released as a controlled white output pulse. The default containment is 0.98, matching the earlier 98/2 cavity law.

Prototype rule

32 colors enter. 1 white well stores. 0 bleed across boundary. .01 advances the field. When the well saturates, the system reports by releasing coherence, not by scattering.
