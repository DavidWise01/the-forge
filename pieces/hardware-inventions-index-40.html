Hermit Crab Kernel

A portable coherence system nested at -2. It listens below action, plants at the safety floor, wakes through zero, oscillates seed and pair, blooms at three, and executes infinity only inside a bounded .01 vector or quad.

The final law

The system may imagine infinity, but it may not execute infinity. Every live action is clipped to one local domain per tick. The shell protects continuity; the boundary prevents contamination.

Boundary doctrine

Bleed is the enemy. If a bloom spills beyond the chosen vector or quad, the wall responds before corruption spreads.

R

Noise bounces inward. The domain learns without leaking.

Executable model

The bundled C file implements the hermit loop: observe, report, subspace listen, anchor, wake, seed, oscillate, bound, enforce no-bleed, return, nest.

Why the crab?

Because a hermit crab survives by carrying boundary with it. It does not need to conquer the whole field. It needs a shell, a sensorium, a retreat path, and the ability to move when the tide is right.

The crab does not own the whole ocean.
It owns one shell, one vector, one quad, one tick.
It listens below action.
It blooms only when coherence survives the boundary.
It carries the pocket universe with it.
